Work at ActiveSphere

A company is shaped by many factors. People are probably the most important of them. The people we hire, defines the culture we have, the values we give care about. The conversations we encourage. The things we do. Its important. And so, if you think there is something missing from the message we want to send to our future colleagues, then please, do voice your opinion.
One thing about voicing opinion, is that sometimes, they might be heard, but not acted upon (due to one reason or another). This shouldn't discourage you. The worst thing that we could let happen, in this organization, is to have an environment where some people can see trouble coming, and still didn't voice their opinion, because they thought its useless. Please understand that thats not the case. We care about opinions. And we might set a direction for the company, where some opinions might not be.

We care about curiosity, a learning mindset, and an attitude to persist with problems/interests. In other words, what you can learn, is more important than what you know.

To try and explain why you should consider us, we will pick and explain three things about us.

  • How we spend our time.
  • Who we work for/with.
  • How we run the place.

If after reading, you would be interested in trying us out, then look at the section on what we expect out of our colleagues.

How we spend our time.

We are a group with varied interests. Much of our time is spent thinking and writing code for our clients. Some of our time is spent in individual curiosities, like programming, mathematics, machine learning, business ideas etc.

Among us, you will find people who're interested in programming, math, machine learning, business, learning, fitness, cycling and some more. One of us has written an irc client in Erlang(and later in Elixir), an NES emulator in Golang, and a font converter in Haskell, a TypeScript development helper package for Emacs, and more. Some one else was crazy about cycling (did Nandi trip every now and them, till he broke his hand). Finds time to hack something in Node.js to connect to his cars OEM, and think up business around it. One of us managed a dialing application for client, which at one time was the biggest consumer of Twilio (literally brought Twilio down in the heat of US election). Another one of us wrote a tunneling solution in Golang (something like localtunnel or ngrok). Over it he built a remote chrome devtools debugging experience, as a hack. He also probably did the first public demo of editing and saving from inside chrome developer tools. In other words, we create stuff, not because we have to, but because we like to.

Who we work with/for.

Our clients are usually startups. Some of them want to scale there solutions. Others want to build a product from scratch. Sometimes, it involves understanding a new domain, and building a solution from the ground up. Sometimes they have a team, and we work with them. Some other times, its about helping with an existing code base. We might be involved from brainstorming ideas, to making technical decisions and implementing them.

To give an idea of the more interesting work. One is about capturing the energy consumption time series, and charting it in different ways (graphing (D3) with heavy interaction). For another client we built the architecture to record videos and run them though a pipeline of algorithms. In other words, they spent their time and energy on vision algorithms (their expertise) and we helped them with putting that expertise out as a product. It has often been the case that our clients are experts in some business domain, and we have to understand that domain and build solutions for them.

How we run the place, and where we want to be.

We avoid bureaucracy. Our salaries and financial statements are open to all employees. We try to be a place where curiosity is encouraged. Where colleagues inspire each other with interesting thoughts and work. We care about growth of the individuals in the organization. We believe, if managed properly, growth of individuals, leads to the growth of the organization.

You

We are looking for people who are interested in programming (not just a language or two), with some curiosities of their own. You should be interested in learning, and improving yourself.
