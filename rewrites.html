Rewriting software to improve performance & scalability

Rewrites should probably be avoided if possible as they can be risky. They've often been known to harm business instead of helping.

That being said, one might consider it if it brings value to the business.

While going for a rewrite, one might have to consider many things like

  • Is the domain well understood
  • Are goals & expectations clear
  • Is there a clear understanding of how the old system works, including issues/bottlenecks
  • Do the new changes impact maintainability?
  • How to get the rewrite out to production quickly & safely

We have some experience rewriting and deploying such software safely. In our experience, it usually involves

  • Understanding and accommodating for risks, early in the project.
  • Breaking down the rewrite into smaller chunks, prioritizing components which help improve the system most while keeping risk at a minimum
  • Choosing the right technologies and tools (learn them if required). In the past we've picked up Elixir, React, Go, Kubernetes, Kafka, Webpack and more to solve specific problems
